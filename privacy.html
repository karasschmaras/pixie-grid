Pixie Grid — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2 June 2026

Pixie Grid is a free, ad-supported game. To show ads we use Google AdMob (the Google Mobile Ads SDK). AdMob may collect device identifiers, usage and interaction data, and crash/performance diagnostics, and — with your consent — may use them to show you personalized ads and to track you across apps and websites. We show Apple’s App Tracking Transparency prompt and, in the EU/EEA/UK, a Google-certified consent screen, and you can always decline. A one-time Remove Ads purchase stops banner and interstitial ads.

1. Who we are

Pixie Grid is made by Karas Games (“we”, “us”). Questions about this policy: karasleon@web.de.

2. Advertising & the data AdMob collects

Pixie Grid is free and supported by ads: a banner, occasional full-screen (interstitial) ads, and optional rewarded videos you choose to watch for in-game help. Ads are served by Google AdMob. To do this, the Google Mobile Ads SDK may collect and process:

  • Device identifiers, including the advertising identifier (IDFA) and other device/diagnostic IDs;
  • Advertising data and product-interaction / usage data (e.g. which ads were shown, taps, video views, app launches);
  • your approximate location derived from IP address;
  • crash and performance diagnostics to keep the ads SDK working.

With your consent, Google may use this data for personalized advertising and to track you across other apps and websites. If you do not consent, you still see ads, but non-personalized (contextual) ones. How Google uses this data is described here: How Google uses information from sites or apps that use its services. You can opt out of personalized Google ads at Google Ads Settings, and manage Google’s data practices via the Google Privacy Policy.

3. Your choices: ATT & EU/UK consent

On first launch we show Apple’s App Tracking Transparency (ATT) prompt; if you choose “Ask App Not to Track,” the IDFA is not used to track you. In the EU/EEA and UK we also show a Google-certified consent message (via Google’s User Messaging Platform) so you can accept or refuse personalized ads under GDPR. You can reopen these choices any time from the in-app Settings. You can always decline — the game stays fully playable.

4. Remove Ads & in-app purchases

Pixie Grid offers optional in-app purchases. A one-time “Remove Ads” purchase removes the banner and interstitial ads (optional rewarded help videos remain available if you choose them). Cosmetic packs are also available. All purchases are processed by Apple through the App Store / StoreKit. We never see your payment details. Use the in-app Restore Purchases control to ask Apple — not us — to re-confirm your entitlements.

5. Data that stays on your device

Your game progress — solved puzzles, your collection and room, settings, streak, and help tokens — is saved locally on your device using Apple’s on-device storage. This game state is not sent to us and is not synced to iCloud; you can erase it by deleting the app. (This is separate from the ad data described above, which is handled by Google AdMob.)

6. Children’s privacy

Pixie Grid is a general-audience game and is not directed to children under 13 (or under 16 where applicable), and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Because the app serves third-party ads via AdMob, it is not intended for child-directed use; please do not let young children use it without supervision. This is consistent with COPPA and equivalent children’s-privacy rules for a general-audience app.

7. Your rights (GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA/CPRA)

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of your personal data, and to opt out of personalized advertising. Because the ad data is collected and controlled by Google, the most direct controls are Google Ads Settings, the EU/UK consent screen, and the ATT prompt; you can also use the Google Privacy Policy controls. For California residents, we do not sell your personal information for money; sharing of identifiers with Google for cross-context behavioral advertising occurs only with your consent, and you can withhold it. Questions: karasleon@web.de.

8. International data transfers

Google is a global provider and may process ad-related data in countries including the United States. Such transfers are governed by Google’s safeguards (e.g. Standard Contractual Clauses) under the Google Privacy Policy.

9. Data retention

We retain no personal data on our own servers (we have none). Ad-related data is retained by Google per its own policies. Local game state remains on your device until you delete it or the app.

10. Changes to this policy

If we change how the app handles data, we will update this policy, change the “Last updated” date, and update the app’s App Store privacy labels before that version ships.

11. Contact

Questions about this policy or your privacy: karasleon@web.de (Karas Games).
